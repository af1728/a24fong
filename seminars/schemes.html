Schemes Learning Seminar

We meet weekly on Wednesdays 10:00-11:00 in MC 5417.

References

Our main reference for the first section is The Geometry of Schemes by David Eisenbud and Joe Harris, specifically Chapters I to III. Here are some additional references which may be helpful:

Varieties

  • Varieties and Their Coordinate Rings. Provides the bare minimum of knowledge about varieties required to start, for anyone who has forgotten (or doesn't know) what varieties are.
  • Miles Reid, Undergraduate Algebraic Geometry.

Cohomology

  • Robin Hartshorne, Algebraic Geometry, chapter IV.
  • Jean-Pierre Serre, Faisceaux algébriques cohérents.

Other General References

  • Robin Hartshorne, Algebraic Geometry.
  • Andreas Gathmann, Class notes "Algebraic Geometry".

Talks

17 Jan 2024 AJ Fong - Affine schemes

Topics covered: The definition of affine schemes, as sets, the Zariski topology, and the structure sheaves. Basic definitions associated with sheaves [Eisenbud-Harris, Section I.1].

24 Jan 2024 AJ Fong - More on sheaves, general schemes

Topics covered: Some more basic notions about sheaves, including stalks. Quasicoherent sheaves of ideals and subschemes. Local rings and some important geometric notions in schemes defined using these [Eisenbud-Harris, Sections I.1.3, I.2.1-2]<./p>

31 Jan 2024 Jacques van Wyk - All about the stalks

Topics covered: The sheafification construction. Kernel and cokernel sheaves [Eisenbud-Harris Section I.1.3]. The image sheaf with its canonical embedding. Exactness of sheaves.
